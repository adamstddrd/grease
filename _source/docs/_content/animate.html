--- title: Animate order: 04 ---

.animate

.animate is a simple system for rapidly creating complex transitions. Create a CSS keyframe animation, and use .animate to apply it to any element and manage when it runs (now, after a delay, or when it scrolls into view). Progressive enhancement is baked in, so you don’t have to worry about leaving your visitors with a broken page if they have js disabled.

--[animation-name] modifiers

.animate ships with a single animation modifier, --fade-in. It’s both a useful animation and a proof of concept for you to follow. The idea here is to give you the tools to create your own animations that are flexible and composable vs. shipping dozens of animations that you probably won't use.

To add a new animation, create your keyframe animation and add a corresponding modifier with the same name in _source/_assets/stylesheets/utilities/animate.css.

--delay modifiers

--delay modifier change your animations to run after a delay instead of immediately. By adding multiple delays on multiple elements, you can compose complex transitions.

animate --fade-in --delay-s

animate --fade-in --delay-m

animate --fade-in --delay-l

animate --fade-in --delay-xl

animate --fade-in --delay-xxl

Animate on scroll

In addition to delaying by a specific amount of time, you can also delay animations until the element is visible in the viewport. Simple move the animation modifier from the class property to a data attribute called data-aos. Simple. You can combine this with the delay modifiers too.

class="animate --delay-xl" data-aos="--fade-in"

--duration modifiers

Any animation modifier you create should include a default duration. The --duration modifiers let you alter the duration on a case-by-case basis.

animate --fade-in --duration-s

animate --fade-in --duration-m

animate --fade-in --duration-l

animate --fade-in --duration-xl

animate --fade-in --duration-xxl
